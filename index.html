About

Building Bridges Between
Technology, Policy, and Impact

For more than fifteen years, I have worked to make emerging technologies serve the public interest. My career began in finance at S&P Global and the African Development Bank, shifted into frontline diplomacy as a U.S. Foreign Service Officer with Top Secret clearance, and has since evolved into a practice centered on advising multilateral institutions on how to govern, deploy, and benefit from AI, FinTech, and digital infrastructure.

I have led digital economy assessments for USAID in El Salvador, directed open banking reform initiatives for the European Union across the Southern Mediterranean, designed governance programs for senior officials with UNDP in Madagascar, and developed AI integration strategies for DAI. My current research, conducted through a PhD at the University of the Basque Country, examines how decentralized organizations reshape governance and business models.

What distinguishes my approach is the combination of institutional credibility, field-level execution, and cross-sector fluency. I operate comfortably in a cabinet minister's office, a blockchain lab, or a university lecture hall, and I bring that versatility to every engagement.
